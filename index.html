I started making jewellery as a hobby about 7/8years ago, this hobby quickly became an obsession to learn the many and varied techniques involved in jewellery making, starting with stringing plastic beads, going on to macramé, kumihimo and traditional pearl knotting, using silk threads and leather.

A whole new world was opened when I discovered gemstones, the many, many, different stones available, the different colours, the different shapes, the different cuts, amazing! I’ve been told I was into bling before bling was even trendy, I have always simply loved jewellery of all sorts! I then added sterling silver chains and findings to my list of must use materials.

I then had the chance to join a silversmith class, I was hooked from the very first! I learnt all I could in class and followed this up with books and internet tutorials, I’m continuing to learn all the time and still loving every minute of it!

All of my pieces are hand made by myself at my workbench at home and I now have my own ‘makers mark’ so every piece of silver jewellery I make I send to the Edinburgh Assay Office for hallmarking. Unless otherwise stated, all my pieces are made with genuine gemstones and 925 sterling silver.
